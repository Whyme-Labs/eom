# EOM — Storyboard

**Format:** 1920×1080
**Audio:** No voiceover. Underscore (warm minimal piano/pad, ~70 BPM) +
sparse SFX. Music is felt, not heard.
**Style basis:** DESIGN.md — cream + ink + antique-gold, Georgia serif for
brand moments, monospaced for numbers and code, no shadows, no neon.
**Captions:** every SCRIPT.md line is one on-screen caption frame in
Georgia 28-32px, ink on cream, fade-through transitions. Captions live
below the action in a 1080→880px content band — top 200px stays for
"editorial header" treatment.

## Global guardrails

- Cream `#FAFAF7` is the base layer of every frame. Never let it disappear.
- Antique gold `#B8860B` accents are rare — at most one gold element per
  beat (the active-tab underline, a numeric callout, the source-span
  marker). Don't gilt the whole frame.
- All numeric callouts are monospaced. Numbers ARE the visual hook.
- No drop shadows. Depth = 1px hairline rules + type weight.
- Transitions between beats are slow page-turn fades (300–500ms). Never
  whip-pan or shake — this is a *long-read journal*, not a launch reel.
- Music swells gently into Beat 3 (the inbound demo, the wedge), drops to
  near-silence on the "0.31× / 69%" callout, resolves on a final piano
  note at the close card.
- SFX: faint typewriter tick on each caption flip; soft paper-rustle on
  beat transitions; a single muted chime when the 69% callout lands.

## Per-beat direction

### BEAT 1 — Hook  (0:00 – 0:15)

**Captions (in order):**
> Markdown was built for storage.
> Not for dialogue.
> Every chat with an AI is a flat byte stream — both sides re-derive what matters every turn.
> So we built a wire format for it.

**Concept.** Open silent on a cream page with the `📰 EOM` mark already
present, off-centre-left. A single hairline gold rule draws across the
screen left→right (SVG stroke-dashoffset) under the headline as the first
caption fades in. Caption text appears typewriter-ticked, one line at a
time. No camera move yet.

**Visual.** Five layers:
1. Cream paper background, very faint horizontal text-grain (1% opacity).
2. The Georgia brand mark `📰 EOM`, 96px, ink.
3. Hairline gold rule, 2px → opacity 60%, animating left→right.
4. Caption stack centred below the rule, fade-in stagger (300ms).
5. Bottom-right meta tag in monospaced grey: `EOM v0.2 · github.com/Whyme-Labs/eom`.

**Camera.** Static frame. Captions are the only motion; let the typography
do the work.

**Transition out.** Page-turn fade (paper slides out leftward 120ms, new
beat slides in rightward, 380ms power2.inOut) on the last caption ("So we
built a wire format for it."). Music swells half a step.

**SFX.** Faint typewriter tick on each caption flip; a single soft chime
when the brand mark first appears.

### BEAT 2 — Outbound (AI → human)  (0:15 – 0:50)

**Captions:**
> EOM is a two-way wire format between humans and models, with attention budgets and source grounding built in.
> One IR. Two lowerings.
> **Outbound** — AI to human.
> The same EOM document projects to a newspaper brief.
> Headline. Lede. Body. Archive. Every claim grounded to a span in the source.
> Twelve formal validators. H1 through H12. This document passes all of them.

**Concept.** We see the live demo for the first time. Hero strip at top of
frame (1920×220), the rest of the frame becomes a stage for the newspaper
render. We pan slowly over the rendered brief, and the harness-pass badge
slides in from the right at the end.

**Visual.** Six layers, all working off real Playwright captures:
1. Top 220px: demo hero strip, real screenshot. The binding tags (`R2, D1
   (32 docs), KV, AI, OpenRouter`) sit muted in the meta line — visible
   but not foregrounded yet (they take centre in Beat 4).
2. Mid-band 220→820px: the newspaper-render iframe content as a still
   image, 1500×600 inset card, soft 1px gold rule top + bottom.
3. Slow Ken-Burns zoom (1.00 → 1.04) over the newspaper render across 12s.
4. Source-span hover overlay: a single block in the brief gets a gold
   underline + tooltip showing `[src:claim-2]` and the source quote — 2s
   pause to read.
5. Right-rail: the 5 tab labels (Newspaper / Context pack / JSON / Harness
   / Ask AI), with Newspaper underlined in gold (active).
6. Final 5s of beat: harness panel slides in (right→centre, 400ms) showing
   the `PASS` badge + metric table.

**Camera.** Slow rightward drift (translateX 0 → -30px) during the Ken
Burns, then settle for the harness reveal.

**Transition out.** Iris into the Ask AI tab — a vignette dark-ring closes
to centre, opens on the inbound demo. 500ms.

**SFX.** Music sustained pad. Paper-rustle on the iris transition. No
percussion yet.

### BEAT 3 — Inbound (human → AI)  (0:50 – 1:55)

**Captions (12 lines, the wedge beat):**
> **Inbound** — human to AI.
> Same IR. Different lowering.
> We ask the same model the same question — with two different contexts.
> Left: raw markdown.
> Right: the EOM context-pack.
> Same answer. Smaller payload.
> **0.31× the tokens.**
> **69% reduction.**
> Across our benchmark — three documents, fifteen questions — EOM cuts input tokens by **52%**.
> The pack is editorially lossy *by design*.
> Tier-A always survives. Tier-C compresses. Tier-D drops.
> High-salience answers preserved. Tail detail demoted by priority, not by accident.

**Concept.** This is the load-bearing beat. We watch the Ask AI tab populate
in real time. The two-column comparison is the visual. When the input-token
delta lands, the page softens, and a giant `0.31×` callout rises into
centre frame.

**Visual.**
1. Demo frame, Ask AI tab. Two cards side-by-side at 900×560 each.
2. The preset question selected: "Which countries topped the medal table by
   gold and how did they compare?" (paris-2024-q3). Reference answer caption
   shows below the dropdown in muted antique-gold tint.
3. Click `Run side-by-side →`. Both spinners. After ~3s the answers
   appear synchronously.
4. The two `Input tokens` metrics animate from `—` to their values via
   a JS counter:
   - Left: `1499` (monospaced 48px, ink)
   - Right: `471` (monospaced 48px, ink), with a `-69%` chip in gold
     animating up alongside.
5. **The Callout** (0:60 – 0:75 of overall video, ~15s mid-beat). Background
   softens to 60% opacity. A giant monospaced `0.31×` (160px, ink) rises
   from below the frame to centre, with `69% reduction` beneath it
   (Georgia italic 48px, muted). Music drops to near-silence. Hold 2s.
   Fade out. Demo frame returns to full opacity.
6. Second callout — the corpus average. The table from
   `data/bench/results/20260510-094128.md` animates in row-by-row
   (3 docs × raw+pack), and a final summary line resolves to:
   `Total: 16,132 → 7,677 tokens · 52% reduction.`
7. **The trade-off line** (last 20s of beat). The 5 questions per doc
   become small circles in a horizontal strip. Three circles fill solid
   gold (Tier-A preserved). Two go hollow (tail-detail dropped). Caption
   "editorially lossy by design" lands as the trade-off resolves.

**Camera.** Mostly static demo frame. The callout phase pulls focus by
softening everything else; no actual zoom.

**Transition out.** Caption "demoted by priority, not by accident." holds
on screen; a thin gold rule draws across the bottom, then everything
fades to cream. 600ms.

**SFX.** A single muted chime when `0.31×` lands. Soft paper-rustle as the
trade-off circles fill. Music returns to full underscore for Beat 4.

### BEAT 4 — Architecture  (1:55 – 2:30)

**Captions:**
> One core IR. Two asymmetric dialects. Shared validator.
> Outbound carries visual primitives — lede, hero, factbox, archive.
> Inbound carries model-context primitives — system intent, role tags, evidence layers, token budgets.
> Same blocks underneath. Different projections out.
> Deployed on the Cloudflare suite — Pages, Workers, R2, D1, KV, Workers AI.

**Concept.** Pure typography + SVG diagram. Step out of the demo, onto a
blank cream page, and draw the architecture from `docs/SPEC-v0.2.md` §1.
The diagram builds in stages.

**Visual.**
1. Cream page. Centred title: `EOM v0.2 — architecture` (Georgia 36px).
2. SVG diagram drawn live with `stroke-dashoffset` over 8s:
   - Top box: `[ Core IR ]` (rounded rect, ink stroke 2px on cream fill).
   - Two branches descending: ` outbound dialect ` left, ` inbound dialect ` right.
   - Lowering targets at the leaves: left side `HTML newspaper · mobile cards
     · slide deck`, right side `LLM context-pack · retrieval payload ·
     tool-call payload`. Each leaf appears with a 200ms fade-up as the
     stroke reaches it.
3. As "Cloudflare suite" caption lands, the 6 binding tags from the demo's
   hero meta (`R2, D1 (32 docs), KV, AI, OpenRouter, Pages+Workers`) drop
   in as small monospaced pill chips along the bottom of the frame, each
   with its CF service mark (R2 / D1 / KV / AI / OpenRouter as text-only
   pill — no logos to license).
4. The diagram and the chip strip never both fully foreground — chips sit
   below the diagram, separated by a 1px gold rule.

**Camera.** Static. The drawing itself is the motion.

**Transition out.** The diagram and chips fade. The cream stays. 400ms.

**SFX.** Underscore continues. A faint pen-on-paper scribble layered low
under the SVG drawing.

### BEAT 5 — Fine-tune (Unsloth track)  (2:30 – 2:50)

**Captions:**
> For the Unsloth track, we fine-tuned Gemma-4-E4B on Modal.
> bf16. Rank-32 LoRA. Thirty epochs. Canonical Unsloth recipe.
> The adapter is the IR frontend — raw text in, EOM out, offline.

**Concept.** A terminal card. Show the Modal output and a couple of
training-loss numbers. The Gemma-4 model name and "Unsloth" both get
gold-tinted highlights, since the track is the structural anchor of the
submission.

**Visual.**
1. Cream background. Centred terminal-card 1400×680, ink-on-cream, 1px
   gold rule, monospaced.
2. Three typed-on-screen lines (each at 30 chars/sec):
   - `$ modal volume ls eom-sft-out | grep gemma4-v5`
   - `eom-sft-adapter-gemma4-v5/`
   - `$ cat scripts/modal_train_gemma4_v5.py | head -16`
3. Then the v5 recipe header excerpt fades in below: `r=32, lora_alpha=32,
   bf16=True, 30 epochs, lr=1e-4`. The phrase `bf16` and `r=32` glow gold
   briefly when their caption line is visible.
4. A small training-loss sparkline (SVG path, 200×40) draws across the
   right side of the card: train loss curve from v5's actual numbers
   (decreasing from ~2.8 to ~0.09).

**Camera.** Static. Caret blink at end of last line for 1s before fade.

**Transition out.** Card fades. Cream stays. 400ms.

**SFX.** Soft typewriter rhythm under the typed lines. The sparkline draws
silently. A single quiet chime when the loss curve completes.

"EOM is for dialogue." Then silence (no further notes) for the URL hold.
